## Words and phrases

**exterior** — on the outside.

**bounded region** — an area that has a limit around it.

**corpus-based** — using many real examples of language.

**contribute** — add something that helps make the whole.

**infer** — understand something that was not said directly.

**compact** — in a small area and close together.

**distribute** — give one or more things to different people.

**accessible** — easy to reach, get, or use.

**conventional** — used and accepted by many speakers.

**interchangeable** — able to change places without changing the meaning.

**semantic map** — a picture that shows how meanings are connected.

**exclusion** — not including someone or something.

**depletion** — using something until little or none remains.

**resolution** — finding an answer and making a problem clear.

**retrieval** — remembering something without looking at the answer.

**reliable** — something you can trust.

**delayed advantage** — a better result found after some time has passed.

**transitive** — used with a person or thing after the verb.

**separable** — allowing the object between the verb and the particle.

**direct object** — the person or thing that the verb acts on.

**pronoun** — a word such as it, them, or him.

**take out** — move something from inside to outside.

**spread out** — move from a close group into a wider area.

**lay out** — arrange things on a surface.

**hand out** — give something to each person in a group.

**point out** — make someone notice something.

**find out** — get information or learn a fact.

**figure out** — understand something or find a solution after thinking.

**get out (information)** — become known when it was private.

**leave out** — not include someone or something.

**rule out** — decide that something is not possible or suitable.

**cut out** — remove something or stop including it.

**run out** — use all of something so none remains.

**wear out** — become unusable through use, or make someone very tired.

**die out** — become less common and finally stop existing.

**work out** — exercise, calculate, develop successfully, or solve.

**sort out** — deal successfully with a problem or confusing situation.
