## Words and phrases

**inner speech** — Words that happen privately in the mind.

**retrieve** — Bring known information out of memory when it is needed.

**mental load** — The amount of information the mind must hold at one time.

**chunk** — A group of words that often travels together as one useful phrase.

**paraphrase** — Say the same basic meaning with different or easier words.

**rehearse** — Practice before the real moment.

**private speech** — Quiet speech to yourself that helps guide a task.

**predict** — Make a reasonable guess about what comes next.

**retell** — Say the main story or message again in your own words.

**native-like** — Similar to a person who learned the language from early childhood.

**the main reason is** — A phrase for introducing the most important reason.

**the next step is** — A phrase for saying what should happen after this.

**keep the meaning moving** — Continue communicating when the best word is missing.

**look it up** — Search for information in a dictionary or another trusted source.

**how about…?** — A phrase for suggesting another choice.

## 10. The Five-Minute English Thought Loop

**Eli:** Minute five: say the thought again. Then change one detail.

**Nora:** If the first thought was asking a doctor about a headache, the related thought might be asking about medicine.

**Eli:** If the first thought was explaining a late project, the related thought might be asking for help.

## 11. A Seven-Day Test For Adults

**Nora:** Here is the seven-day plan. Five minutes each day. We are not promising fluency in one week.

**Nora:** Day three: one familiar routine aloud. Making coffee, starting work, driving home, or preparing dinner. Use simple English for two minutes, check two gaps, and repeat.

**Eli:** Day four: describe one picture. Then hide it and retrieve the description from memory. If you do not experience a strong inner voice, use a quiet voice or write three English notes first.

**Nora:** Day five: missing-word rescue. Practice “It’s a thing that…,” “It’s the place where…,” and “It means…” Use three words you often forget.

**Eli:** Day six: prediction and retelling. Listen to a short English clip. Predict one next idea. Then pause and retell the main point in two or three sentences.

**Nora:** Day seven: one adult question. “What should our team change?” “Why do I want this job?” “What do I need to ask the doctor?” Or “How can my family solve this problem?”

**Eli:** Record a two-minute answer. Check one meaning gap and one English gap. Then answer again.

**Nora:** Measure four things. How long did it take to begin? Did you complete the main message? Did you recover when a word was missing? Was the second attempt easier?

**Nora:** And do not use dreams in English as your final test.
